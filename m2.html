summer

Summer is the hottest of the four temperate seasons, falling after spring and before autumn.

winter

Winter is the coldest season of the year in polar and temperate zones (winter does not occur in most of the tropical zone).

rainny

The wet season (sometimes called the rainy season) is the time of year when most of a region's average annual rainfall occurs.


content

A season is a division of the year[1] marked by changes in weather, ecology, and amount of daylight. On Earth, seasons result from Earth's orbit around the Sun and Earth's axial tilt relative to the ecliptic plane.

spring

Spring is one of the four temperate seasons, following winter and preceding summer.

autumn

Autumn, also known as fall in North American English,[1] is one of the four temperate seasons.
